Sports Fiction

Genre Color: Dark Red/Maroon

These stories revolve around team or individual sports, training for sports, and have major themes about sportsmanship.



The Crossover

Interest Level: 5th-8th Grade
Written by Kwame Alexander

Fourteen-year-old twin basketball stars Josh and Jordan wrestle with highs and lows on and off the court as their father ignores his declining health.



Matt Christopher: Snowboard Champ

Interest Level: 3rd-6th Grade
Written by Paul Mantell
Part of the Matt Christopher series

Matt Harper is sent to live with his uncle while his mother is assigned an overseas government job, but finds he must compete with his new school's most popular boy and best snowboarder while trying to dispel damaging rumors about himself and his mom.



The Underdogs

Interest Level: 5th-8th Grade
Written by Mike Lupica

Small but fast twelve-year-old Will Tyler, an avid football player in the down-and-out town of Forbes, Pennsylvania, takes matters into his own hands to try and finance the city's football team, giving the whole community hope in the process.



BMX Bully

Interest Level: 5th-8th Grade
Written by Jake Maddox; Illustrated by Sean Tiffany
Part of the Jake Maddox series

Matt's chances of making the Evergreen Racing Team are seriously threatened when cheating Tyler moves into town, in an illustrated novel for reluctant readers.



Beach Volleyball is No Joke

Interest Level: K-3rd Grade
Written by Anita Yasuda; Illustrated by Jorge Santillan
Part of the Victory School Superstars series

Instead of trying to learn beach volleyball, Tyler spends a week at camp pulling practical jokes, only to upset his teammates.



Stink and the Ultimate Thumb-Wrestling Smackdown

Interest Level: K-3rd Grade
Written by Megan McDonald; Illustrated by Peter Reynolds

After second-grader Stink gets an unsatisfactory grade in physical education, his parents tell him he must play a sport and so he masters thumb wrestling, as seen on a sports channel.
